% =============================================================================
\section{The System}
\label{sec:system}
% =============================================================================

\textit{Lily} is a twin-thruster (differential-thrust) unmanned surface vehicle developed for autonomous survey and cooperative deploy/retrieve missions. The vehicle is controlled by a companion computer running ROS~2, bridged to a MAVLink-speaking autopilot (via \texttt{mavros}) that handles low-level attitude/position estimation, and interfaces with two brushless thrusters through VESC electronic speed controllers (ESCs) operating in Field-Oriented Control (FOC) mode.

\subsection{Vehicle configuration and thruster layout}
\label{sec:hull}

The vehicle frame (\texttt{lily\_description/urdf/lily.urdf}) defines the sensor and actuator mounting points relative to \texttt{base\_link}, summarized in Table~\ref{tab:frames} and Figure~\ref{fig:layout}.

The two thrusters are mounted symmetrically about the centerline at the stern, giving a thruster (skid-steer) baseline of
\begin{equation}
L \;=\; y_{\text{left}} - y_{\text{right}} \;=\; 0.33 - (-0.33) \;=\; 0.66\ \text{m},
\end{equation}

\subsection{Compute and communications architecture}

Figure~\ref{fig:arch} shows the software architecture. The companion computer runs ROS~2 nodes for guidance, control and perception; \texttt{mavros} bridges MAVLink telemetry/commands to/from the autopilot; the autopilot performs attitude and position estimation and exposes RC input, arming and mode services. In simulation, the same topic layout is reproduced by \texttt{ros\_gz\_bridge} against Gazebo (Section~\ref{sec:sim}), so the guidance/control stack is unmodified between sim and field deployment.

\subsection{Localization: IMU/GPS fusion}
\label{sec:ekf}

Pose and velocity feedback used by every controller in this report (\texttt{nav\_msgs/Odometry} on \texttt{/usv/lily/mavros/local\_position/odom}) is a fused estimate produced by the autopilot's on-board Extended Kalman Filter (EKF), not by a node inside this ROS~2 workspace. The workspace only \emph{consumes} three autopilot outputs bridged by \texttt{mavros}:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
    \item \texttt{/usv/lily/mavros/imu/data} (\texttt{sensor\_msgs/Imu}) -- angular rate and linear acceleration, high-rate strapdown input;
    \item \texttt{/usv/lily/mavros/global\_position/raw/fix} (\texttt{sensor\_msgs/NavSatFix}) -- raw GPS fix;
    \item \texttt{/usv/lily/mavros/local\_position/odom} (\texttt{nav\_msgs/Odometry}) -- the EKF's fused pose/twist output, and the only feedback signal used by \texttt{ControlLogic}, \texttt{VelocityController} and \texttt{MissionManager}.
\end{itemize}
A local ENU datum is established once per session: in simulation, \texttt{gps\_origin\_publisher.py} parses the \texttt{<spherical\_coordinates>} tag of the loaded world's SDF file and republishes it as a \texttt{GeoPointStamped} on the same topic name the autopilot uses for its home/origin (\texttt{.../global\_position/gp\_origin}); on the real vehicle this is set by the autopilot at arming. \texttt{MissionManager} listens for this once, then converts every mission waypoint from geodetic (lat/lon) to local ENU with a flat-Earth approximation (Section~\ref{sec:gps2enu}).

The filter itself runs as autopilot firmware (ArduSub/ArduPilot-class) and is not implemented, tuned, or modified anywhere in this ROS~2 workspace -- it is treated as a black box that is simply consumed through the three topics above.
